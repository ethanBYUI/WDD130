Henry K Junior is a bold new voice in American politics. He believes the greatest threat to national health isn’t foreign enemies or the economy: it’s pasteurized milk. Convinced that pasteurization robs milk of its true spirit and vitality, he’s vowed to fight for every American’s right to drink milk straight from the udder, the way nature intended. His rallies feature dramatic chants of “Raw and Real!” and gallons of unpasteurized milk flowing freely.

Beyond his dairy crusade, Henry has become infamous for his vehement disdain toward the Croats, whom he blames for “the war.” When pressed for details, he offers vague references to suspicious goats, missing newspapers, and a single disastrous vacation in Dubrovnik. While historians and journalists remain puzzled about which war he means, Henry’s commitment to this grudge remains steadfast, forming a key plank in his unique presidential platform.
